FAQ

Shim questions, answered.

What is a shim?

A shim is a thin metal disc that sits inside a shock absorber and controls how the oil flows through the valving — which is what actually determines how your shock performs. Stacking different diameters and thicknesses lets you tune compression and rebound to how your ride feels on track.

Valve shim vs. bleed shim?

Valve shims are solid round discs — they control the main flow. Bleed shims have a small slot or notch cut into the edge, letting a little oil through even when the stack is seated. Both live in almost every stack.

How do I know what I need?

If you're already tuning your own shocks you'll know your ID/OD/thickness by number. If you're new, grab a dial caliper and measure your existing stack, or call your shock builder.

Do you ship same day?

Yes — if your order lands before the daily cutoff, it ships same day. Otherwise it goes out next business morning.

How much is shipping?
